## The Brief

Velvet Threads sells premium Indian ethnic wear — lehengas, sarees, and fusion pieces at the ₹8,000–₹80,000 price point. Their existing Shopify store was using a generic theme that made their product look like it belonged on a bargain marketplace. They were getting traffic but not conversions.

The brief was clear: the store needs to feel as premium as the product.

## The Problem with Generic Themes

When you're selling a ₹40,000 lehenga, the default Shopify product page isn't doing you any favours. No context. No story. No way to show how the dupatta drapes, what the embroidery looks like under event lighting, or how to style the full look.

The other big issue: their customers couldn't find what they wanted. Filtering by colour, fabric, occasion, and price simultaneously was impossible. Customers were leaving because navigation was frustrating.

## What We Built

### The Visual Experience

Every product page is now a proper editorial spread. Full-bleed imagery, a zoom viewer that shows embroidery detail, a video section for drape and movement, and a "styled by occasion" section at the bottom.

We rebuilt the colour selector to show actual fabric swatches — not tiny squares, but large, accurate colour chips rendered from the product photography.

### The Wishlist System

Persistent across sessions (Shopify customer account + localStorage fallback), shareable via link or WhatsApp, and deeply integrated with Klaviyo for abandoned wishlist flows. 34% of users who share a wishlist link have an item purchased within 7 days.

### Complete the Look

The hardest part of the build. We used Shopify Metafields to create a "collection" tag system — each product can reference 2–3 complementary pieces. When a customer views a lehenga, they see the matching dupatta, jewellery, and bag. One-click add all to cart. AOV went up 28% within the first month.

## The Result

The new store launched on a Thursday. By Monday, Sarah's inbox was full of messages from customers complimenting the website specifically. Revenue was up 30% month-over-month.
