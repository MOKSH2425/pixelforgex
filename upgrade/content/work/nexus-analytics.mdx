## The Brief

Nexus Fintech came to us with a painful problem: their existing dashboard was a patchwork of third-party tools that didn't talk to each other. Their enterprise clients were complaining about latency, their data team was drowning in manual exports, and their sales team couldn't demo the product without it visibly struggling.

They needed a ground-up rebuild. Fast.

## The Challenge

The core technical challenge was **live data at scale**. Nexus handles over 50,000 transactions per minute at peak, and their clients needed to see this data update in real time — not on a 30-second polling interval, not on refresh. *Live.*

The secondary challenge was **multi-tenancy**. 200+ enterprise clients, each with different data sets, different permission models, different branding requirements. The architecture had to handle this cleanly without becoming a spaghetti of conditional logic.

## What We Built

### The Data Layer

We designed a PostgreSQL schema with full row-level security so every query is automatically scoped to the requesting client. No "WHERE client_id = ?" scattered across 400 queries — the database enforces isolation at the foundation.

Redis sits in front of Postgres for the live feeds — we fanout incoming transaction events through Redis pub/sub channels, which our WebSocket server subscribes to and pushes to connected clients in under 80ms.

### The Dashboard

12 custom chart types built on top of Recharts with a D3.js data transformation layer. The charts are fully interactive — drill down, cross-filter, time-range scrubbing. Each enterprise client can customise their layout, save views, and schedule automated PDF exports.

### The Admin Panel

4-level RBAC: Super Admin, Org Admin, Analyst, Read-only. Permissions are enforced at the API level (not just in the UI), with full audit logging of every action.

## The Result

The rebuild took 10 weeks from kick-off to production. Load time went from 8 seconds to 1.2 seconds. Their CTO told us the new dashboard was the single biggest talking point in their next fundraising deck.
